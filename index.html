    • FAQs  

    • P60s

      Where can I find my P60s?

      Your employer would have sent via email or uploaded to your employee HR software at the end of each tax year (by the 31st of May). If you have trouble locating them, then ask your employer for copies or contact HMRC.

       

      Pensions

      What is a 'relief at source' pension?

      'Relief at source' means your contributions are taken from your pay after tax has already been taken.

      Under the ‘relief at source’ system, your pension provider claims basic rate tax relief (of 20%) on your personal contributions and adds that to your pension pot.

      How can I tell if my pension is 'relief at source'?

      'Relief at source' pensions will show these basic rate tax rate contributions on your pension statement.

      Where can I find my pension statements?

      You can find these by logging into your pension dashboard. You will want to total contributions in each tax year. You can always contact your pension provider to request your statements. Here's an example request.

      “Please provide me with the total amount of personal contributions made to my pension for the last 4 tax years ending 5th April 2019, 2020, 2021 and 2022? Please exclude any employer contributions made.”

  • Do you qualify for pension tax relief?

  • You can claim tax relief on your personal contributions to a registered pension scheme if you paid them before you reached age 75 and have:

    • been a UK resident at some time during the tax year
    • resident in the UK at some time during the preceding five tax years when you joined the pension scheme

  • Pension contributions

    Supports 'relief at source' pensions only

  • You will need the total contributions you have made to any personal pensions for each of the 4 tax years you are claiming.

    Do not include any amounts for:

    • personal term assurance contributions
    • your employer’s own contributions
    • contributions taken from your pay before it was taxed

    Where can I find my pension statements?

    You can find these by logging into your pension dashboard and selecting the tax year contributions you are claiming against. If you have trouble getting the value of your contribution, ask your pension provider the following:

    “Please can you provide me with the total amount of personal contributions made to my pension for the last 4 tax years ending 5th April 2019, 2020, 2021 and 2022? Please exclude any employer contributions made.”
